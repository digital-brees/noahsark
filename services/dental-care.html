Dental Care

Dog & Cat Dental Care in Williamsburg

Your pet's mouth affects their whole-body health, and dental disease is easy to miss. At our Williamsburg hospital, we handle everything from routine cleanings to advanced problems — so small issues never become painful ones.

What happens during a dental cleaning?

Because pets won't hold still for a thorough cleaning, dental procedures are done under anesthesia. We use ultrasonic power scalers and hand instruments to remove tartar from the teeth, both above and below the gum line, where disease often hides.

Catching problems early gives us more treatment options, which is why we recommend a dental check-up once a year.

Is anesthesia safe for my pet's dental work?

Yes, and we take careful steps to keep it that way. Before any dental procedure, we perform a full physical exam and run bloodwork to make sure your pet is a good candidate for anesthesia.

During the procedure we monitor oxygen levels, carbon dioxide, ECG, blood pressure, heart rate, and temperature the entire time, and we use local anesthetic injections to reduce pain if an extraction is needed.

Will my pet need a tooth extraction?

Sometimes, but our goal is always for your pet to keep as many teeth as possible. When a tooth is too diseased to save, an extraction relieves pain and stops periodontal disease from spreading to nearby teeth and tissue.

Caught early, many dental problems can be treated without extraction, which is one more reason routine dental check-ups matter.

What are the warning signs of dental problems?

Contact us if you notice chronic bad breath, drooling, bleeding in the mouth, dropping food while eating, or a yellowish-brown crust along the gum line. Other signs include missing, loose, or broken teeth, shying away when you touch the mouth, or loss of appetite and weight.

Because pets are so good at hiding oral pain, these signs are worth acting on early.
