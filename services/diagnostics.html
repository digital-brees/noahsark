Diagnostics & Imaging

Diagnostics & Imaging for Pets in Williamsburg

When something's wrong, fast and accurate answers matter. Our in-house lab and digital imaging diagnose many conditions during your visit here in Williamsburg, instead of waiting days for outside results — so we can start treatment sooner.

What can in-house lab work tell us about my pet?

A blood or urine sample reveals a lot that an exam alone can't. A complete blood count (CBC) checks red and white cells and platelets, which tells us about immune function, anemia, infection, and clotting. Blood chemistries show how the liver, kidneys, and other organs are working, along with electrolytes and hormone levels.

We also run urinalysis and fecal exams to check for infection, kidney issues, diabetes, and intestinal parasites. Because the lab is in-house, we often have answers the same visit.

When would you recommend bloodwork?

We commonly recommend lab work at a puppy or kitten's first visit, during annual and senior wellness exams, before surgery or anesthesia, before starting certain medications, and any time a pet seems "off" without an obvious cause. Pre-surgical bloodwork helps us check liver and kidney function so we can choose the safest anesthesia plan.

For senior pets, routine labs often catch treatable problems early, and we frequently see older pets bounce back once an issue is found and addressed.

What is digital radiology, and why does it matter?

Digital x-rays capture images of your pet's body and send them straight to a computer, with no waiting for film to develop. If an image isn't quite right, we can see it immediately and retake it on the spot.

The images are clearer and more detailed than traditional film, we can zoom and adjust them for a better look, and they emit less radiation. We use digital radiology for both dental views and the whole body, helping us spot fractures, joint changes, and sometimes foreign objects.

Do you offer other imaging and cardiac diagnostics?

Yes. Alongside digital x-rays, we use diagnostic imaging to look more closely at specific concerns, and we can perform cardiac workups when a pet shows signs of heart disease. If your pet needs imaging or testing beyond what's done during a routine exam, we'll explain what we're looking for and why before we proceed.
