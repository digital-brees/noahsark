Dog Behavior and Training: A Compassionate, Effective Approach

Behavior problems are one of the most common, and most preventable, reasons dogs lose their homes. The encouraging part is that most unwanted behaviors respond well to early, humane training, and your veterinary team here in Williamsburg can help you get there. We never recommend harsh or punishing methods; the goal is a stronger bond, not a fearful dog.

Which behaviors can training help with?

Training can address the issues owners struggle with most, including aggression, excessive barking or howling, destructive chewing, food guarding, mouthing and nipping, and separation anxiety. Aggression and food guarding in particular won't resolve on their own and tend to worsen without help, so they're worth addressing sooner rather than later. Separation anxiety sometimes needs medication alongside training to ease a dog's stress.

Why is puppy training so important?

The behaviors a puppy learns early are the ones that stick. Puppies move through key social and behavioral milestones between about 8 and 12 weeks of age, so starting training around 7 to 8 weeks sets your dog up to become a well-adjusted adult. It's far easier to shape good habits early than to undo ingrained ones later, which is why we recommend puppy training, often beginning at the first puppy wellness visit, for every new dog.

When is a behavior change actually a medical issue?

Sometimes what looks like "bad behavior" is a pet telling you something hurts. A sudden change in temperament, new aggression, or excessive whining or howling can signal pain, anxiety, or an underlying medical problem. If your dog's behavior changes noticeably, it's worth an exam to rule out a health cause before assuming it's purely behavioral.
