Does Your Pet Have Allergies? Signs, Testing, and Relief

Allergies rarely threaten a pet's life, but they make them miserable, and they're easy to mistake for something else. Many owners in Williamsburg come in worried about a serious illness and find the real culprit is an allergy. The good news: while allergies can't be cured, they can almost always be managed so your pet is comfortable again.

What do allergies look like in pets?

Most allergy symptoms show up on the skin. Watch for constant licking, scratching, or chewing at the same spots, rubbing against furniture or the floor, fur loss or irritated skin, and recurring ear or skin infections. Some pets also sneeze or wheeze. Because that itching can lead to secondary infections, earlier treatment is usually easier and less expensive than waiting.

Why do pets develop allergies?

Many allergic pets are simply born with a less protective skin barrier, which lets everyday allergens slip in and trigger an immune overreaction. Triggers fall into a few groups: contact allergens like grasses, pollen, dust, and mold; flea bites; food (usually a protein); and things they inhale. Most allergic pets react to more than one thing, and their triggers can shift as their environment changes.

How does allergy testing work?

First we rule out other causes of itchy skin, like fleas, mites, and yeast or bacterial infections. If allergies still look like the root cause, testing comes next. A blood test screens for a wide range of environmental allergens and is the most common option. A skin test, done under sedation, can pinpoint triggers very precisely. Food allergies are the exception, they're best identified with a strict elimination diet over several weeks rather than a test. Our in-house diagnostics help us get to an answer faster.

How are allergies treated?

Treatment is tailored to your pet and usually combines a few approaches: anti-itch medications (including options like Apoquel), medicated baths to wash off allergens and soothe the skin, omega-3 fatty acids, and for the right cases, allergy shots that gradually retrain the immune system. If food is the trigger, a special diet does the work, and good parasite prevention and nutrition support the whole plan. We'll find the combination that gives your pet the most relief.
